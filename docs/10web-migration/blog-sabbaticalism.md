# Sabbaticalism

**Published:** October 31, 2025

Tim Mitchell chronicles his journey after being laid off from Microsoft in late 2023, when he decided to take a year-long sabbatical. What began as genuine time away from work evolved into deep exploration of artificial intelligence, ultimately leading to nine months of intensive study, software development, and client work on data and AI strategies.

## AI's Transformative Impact

Mitchell expresses initial skepticism about technological transformation claims, but his immersion in AI details has shifted his perspective. His background in machine learning-driven personalization and data infrastructure positioned him for this collision with AI advancement.

## The Enterprise Software Shift

Mitchell references former Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella's podcast comments about business application architecture. Nadella suggested that AI agents will consolidate business logic, making traditional backend discrimination unnecessary. As one analysis noted, this approach renders user interface considerations potentially "extraneous" when humans aren't direct system users.

The author agrees that strategic data thinking will matter more than specific agent frameworks for practical AI success.

## Product Management's New Relevance

Industry experts argue that product management capabilities are increasingly valuable in AI deployment. A related concept—"context engineering"—essentially functions as virtual product management for AI systems, including components like product requirement prompts.

Mitchell notes parallels between traditional product management (gathering requirements, planning solutions, collaborating with colleagues) and modern context engineering. He's actively exploring whether these developments represent tailwinds for tech product managers or harbinger challenges ahead, committing to document his findings.
